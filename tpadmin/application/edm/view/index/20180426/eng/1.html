Fast Facts on Emotional Eating

By: Sprout Lifestyle

For many people, emotional eating (or stress eating) is a real issue, and many aren’t aware they are doing it nor the implications.
What is Stress?
Stress is a state of the body and not necessarily always negative. In fact, our body and some people do well with some stress as an energy boost and a motivator. Many experience stress due to work, lack of time, and social and family pressures among many other things. Stress over a long period of time is unhealthy and can lead to making poor food choices.
When we are stressed, adults and children tend to want to eat and drink the following things:

- Sugar found in soft drinks, chocolates, cakes, cookies, and snacks

- Sugar in fresh and dried fruit

- Carbohydate-rich and fried foods

- Desserts

- Wine, beer, alcohol

- Spicy foods

- (smoke cigarettes)

There is a good reason for wanting to eat all these foods too: they relax us, seemingly “calm” us down, and make our taste buds happy. However, eating these types of food isn’t healthy as they are acidic to the body, often very addictive, and most can do harm to our organs especially to the stomach, small and large intestines, liver, and colon. .
External stressors from people, our children, and tolerating daily inconvenieces like traffic can cause us to eat food that tenses up the body and cause more stress. This in turn starts a vicious cycle that makes us want to eat the foods listed above that “relax” us.
Many of these foods are processed, high in salt, and are caloric like meat, eggs, cheese, dairy products (milk/yoghurt/cream), baked goods, and deep-fried foods. It would be useful to do an assessment of how often you eat these foods as they most likely are influencing your stress levels.
What is Mindless Eating?
This is when you buy fast food, eat on the way to work or at your desk, eat too quickly, and in general, not truly thinking what you are putting into your mouth. Sound familiar? Most of us are guilty of this.
So what can you do? Eat with awareness. Order food and buy ingredients with health in mind and as your number one priority. Learn and observe the impact of different foods on your digestion and mood. Remember that what you eat directly affects your general well-being.
What Should We Do and Eat?
Our bodies need real, unprocessed foods. Increase your daily intake of:

- Fresh seasonal fruits and vegetables

- Nuts, seeds, fresh and dried beans

- Tofu and sea vegetables

- Whole grains like brown and black rice, buckwheat and millet (and their noodle, bread and snack products)

Drink plenty of water and fresh juice, and limit coffee and sweetened teas. Get more sleep and do more exercise.
How to Start
Today, start by increasing your intake of real whole foods. An easy way to begin is to try or add one new vegetable every week. Experiment with whole grains like brown or black rice by first mixing with your regular white rice. Slow down and savor every bite of food. A little more awareness of your eating habits and making small changes is the start of making big impacts on your stress levels and overall mood!
